Generate an SAT Writing question that tests the ability to combine ideas effectively.

Provide:
- Notes or bullet points with related information (2-4 related but distinct points)
- A clear context for why this synthesis is needed (specific type of essay, report, or writing task)

The question should:
- Ask which option "most effectively" combines the given information
- Test understanding of emphasis, flow, and logical connection between ideas
- Present answer choices that vary in their effectiveness at synthesizing the information
- Include distractors that may be grammatically correct but fail to establish clear relationships between ideas, create awkward emphasis, or result in unclear logical flow

**Critical Requirements:**

**Avoid Explicit Signaling:** Do NOT use phrases that directly tell students what to emphasize or look for (e.g., "emphasize the paradox," "highlight the transformation," "stress the contrast"). The rhetorical goal should be implicit in the context, requiring students to infer what matters most.

**Create Competitive Distractors:** Each distractor must:
- Include substantively different content selections, not just structural rearrangements of identical information
- Represent plausible but flawed interpretations of the synthesis goal
- Emphasize legitimately relevant aspects of the information (not obviously irrelevant details)
- Avoid being identifiable through simple phrase-matching with the prompt

**Ensure Genuine Difficulty Calibration:**
- For difficulty 1-2: Distractors may have more obvious flaws, but still require comparing multiple reasonable options
- For difficulty 3-5: Distractors should represent sophisticated misinterpretations where students must evaluate subtle differences in emphasis, logical flow, or completeness of synthesis
- The correct answer should not be the only choice containing a specific key phrase or concept from the notes

**Require Multi-Step Reasoning:** Students should need to:
- Analyze the rhetorical situation to determine unstated priorities
- Evaluate how different information selections serve different purposes
- Compare competing legitimate approaches to synthesis
- Make judgment calls about relative effectiveness, not identify obvious matches

**Vary Content Across Choices:** Answer choices should differ meaningfully in WHAT information they prioritize and HOW they connect it, not merely in sentence structure or transitional phrases.
